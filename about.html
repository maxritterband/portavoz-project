Our Story

About Portavoz

A student-led initiative bringing the world's literary voices to English-language readers, one translation at a time.

What is The Portavoz Project?

Portavoz — Spanish for "spokesperson" — is a literary translation archive born at the University of Virginia. We publish original translations by undergraduate students, working under faculty supervision to bring exceptional works of world literature into English for the first time, or to offer fresh new readings of beloved classics.

We believe that literary translation is not merely a technical exercise but a creative act of cultural bridge-building. Every translation in our archive reflects months of careful reading, research, and revision.

The project is open to students translating from any language, and we welcome translations of poetry, short fiction, essays, and excerpts from longer works.

What we look for

  • Rigorous fidelity to the meaning and spirit of the source text
  • Fluid, literary English that reads as an original work
  • A thoughtful translator's note situating the work and the translation choices
  • Faculty endorsement and mentorship throughout the process
  • A short biography of the original author
